Welcome Message

Welcome — Watch This First

Priority Tier One

Week One — Just These Three Things

This is the entire job for week one. Not the recipe library, not the glossary, not every guide in the hub — just these three. Everything else is here when you need it, not before.

The Only Thing That Matters Right Now

Hit your macros, track your waking temperature every morning, and show up to your programmed training sessions. That's it. That's the whole job for the next seven days.

1. Hit your nutrition targets. You don't need to understand the full science yet — just hit the numbers your coach has given you. Use the Recipe Library to find 4-5 easy meals and repeat them. Repetition in week one is a feature, not a failure.
2. Track your waking temperature. Every morning, before getting out of bed. Log it in the Temperature Tracker. This is your single most important feedback metric — start the habit now.
3. Show up to your training sessions. Exactly as programmed. Don't add to it, don't skip it. If you're unsure on form for anything, that's completely normal in week one — flag it in your check-in.
Complete your first Weekly Check-In at the end of the week. Open the Check-In form →

Ignore For Now

  • The full Ignition Protocol science — you'll read this in week 2-4, not now
  • Supplements — not urgent in week one, comes in the next tier
  • The Stress Management System — bookmark it, you won't need it unless something specific comes up
  • 1RM calculators, HRV tools, mobility work — all genuinely useful, all genuinely not urgent yet

Priority Tier Two

Week 2-4 — Understand the Why

The three habits from week one should be running on autopilot by now. This tier is about understanding why you're doing what you're doing — which makes it much easier to stay consistent when motivation dips.

The Only Thing That Matters Right Now

Understand the system you're inside of, and start paying attention to your trends — not just logging data, but actually noticing whether your waking temp and training numbers are moving in the right direction.

1. Read the Ignition Protocol guide. Now that the habit is running, understand the science behind it. Read it here →
2. Read Training & Recovery Over 40 so you understand the structure behind your programme — including why form comes before load. Read it here →
3. Set up your supplement stack. See Supplements: What Actually Works for the exact daily protocol.
Log your 1RMs for your main lifts so your training weights are properly dialled in. Open the 1RM Calculator →
Check in on your waking temperature trend — is it moving? Flat? Don't panic either way, just note it in your check-in.
If you're feeling overwhelmed or wired, run through the Stress Management System — start with the quick check-in at the top.
Keep completing your Weekly Check-In — be honest about what's working and what isn't.

Ignore For Now

  • Bloodwork 101 and DEXA results — relevant once you've actually got results to interpret, not before
  • Understanding TRT — not a week 2-4 conversation for almost anyone
  • Mobility deep-dive — useful, but the daily basics matter more than the targeted work right now

Priority Tier Three

Month Two Onward — Refine & Go Deeper

By now the core habits should feel close to automatic. This is where you start refining the details and using the deeper resources in the hub — not because they weren't important before, but because they're far more useful once the fundamentals are locked in.

The Only Thing That Matters Right Now

Consistency under real life — travel, bad weeks, busy periods — and starting to use the data you've built up (DEXA, bloodwork, training logs) to refine the programme with your coach.

1. Get a DEXA scan if you haven't already, and learn to read it properly. Understanding Your DEXA Results →
2. Get bloodwork done if it's due, and bring questions to your coach rather than self-diagnosing. Bloodwork 101 →
3. Read the Mental Game guide — this is where the all-or-nothing thinking that derails most men tends to show up. Read it here →
Read Healthy Lifestyle Habits and dial in sleep, alcohol, and environment design. Read it here →
Build a daily mobility habit if you haven't already — non-negotiable for longevity. Read it here →
If non-standard hours are part of your life, adapt the principles to your schedule. Shift Work & Non-Standard Hours →
